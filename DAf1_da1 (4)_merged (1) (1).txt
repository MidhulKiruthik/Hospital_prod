Abstract

Modern hospitals operate under high variability in patient arrivals and
doctor availability. Existing cloud healthcare systems rely on static
scheduling and batch analytics, leading to decision-making latency.
This project proposes a cloud-native hospital operations platform that
reduces latency using compute-driven scheduling, real-time workload
forecasting, and asynchronous AI-based clinical summarization.
The system adopts an event-driven architecture with distributed
background workers, non-blocking APIs, and enforced observability,
security, and disaster recovery guarantees.

Offline analytics

Blocking workflows

No existing solution provides continuous state computation with

Conclusion

This project demonstrated the design of a cloud-native hospital operations
platform focused on reducing decision-making latency in appointment
scheduling and clinical workflows.
By combining compute-driven scheduling, real-time workload forecasting,
and asynchronous AI-based clinical summarization, the system improves
operational efficiency while maintaining scalability, security, and fault
tolerance.
The proposed architecture is cloud-agnostic, event-driven, and suitable for
real-world hospital environments with high variability and strict reliability
requirements.
